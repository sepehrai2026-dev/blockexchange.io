Guide

How to choose a crypto exchange

Security first

Check if the exchange has been hacked before and how they handled it. Look for proof of reserves, cold storage policies, and insurance on custodied assets. Two-factor authentication should be mandatory, not optional.

Understand the fee structure

Maker/taker fees are just the start. Watch for deposit fees, withdrawal fees, spread markups on "simple buy" interfaces, and conversion fees between currencies. Some exchanges offer fee discounts for holding their native token or trading at higher volumes.

Regulatory compliance matters

Regulated exchanges provide deposit insurance, clear tax reporting, and legal recourse if something goes wrong. Unregulated offshore exchanges may offer more coins and higher leverage, but at significantly higher risk.

Consider your use case

Casual buyers need a simple interface and fiat on-ramps. Active traders need advanced order types, charting tools, and API access. DeFi users need good withdrawal options and support for multiple networks.
